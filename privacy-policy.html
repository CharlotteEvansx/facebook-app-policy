Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 9, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how RareFoot (“we”, “our”, or “us”) collects, uses, stores, shares, and protects information when you use our website, application, tools, or services connected with https://rarefoot.com.

1. Information We Collect

Depending on how you use our services and what permissions you authorize, we may collect the following types of information:

  • Account information: such as your name, email address, profile information, business name, or contact information that you provide directly to us.
  • Meta platform information: if you connect your Facebook, Instagram, Messenger, Business Manager, Page, ad account, or other Meta-related assets, we may access information that you authorize through Meta’s permissions, such as account IDs, Page IDs, business asset IDs, public profile information, page information, message-related data, campaign or advertising data, and related metadata.
  • Technical information: such as IP address, browser type, device type, operating system, log data, referring URLs, pages visited, and interaction data.
  • Communications: if you contact us, we may collect the content of your messages and our responses.

2. How We Use Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To provide, operate, and maintain our website, app, and related services.
  • To authenticate users and connect authorized Meta accounts, Pages, ad accounts, or business assets.
  • To perform actions requested by users, such as managing connected assets, retrieving analytics, processing messages, or supporting business workflows.
  • To provide customer support and respond to user inquiries.
  • To monitor, secure, debug, and improve our services.
  • To comply with applicable laws, platform policies, and security requirements.

3. Meta Platform Data

If you authorize our app to access data from Meta platforms, we only access and process the information necessary to provide the features you request. We do not sell Meta user data. We do not use Meta platform data for unauthorized purposes.

Access to Meta platform data is subject to the permissions granted by the user and to Meta’s applicable platform terms and policies. You may revoke app access through your Meta, Facebook, Instagram, or Business settings where applicable.

4. Sharing of Information

We do not sell your personal information. We may share information only in the following cases:

  • With service providers that help us operate, host, secure, or maintain our services.
  • With Meta or other platforms when necessary to provide authorized integrations.
  • When required by law, regulation, legal process, or government request.
  • To protect the rights, safety, and security of our users, our business, or others.
  • In connection with a business transfer, such as a merger, acquisition, or asset sale.

5. Data Retention

We retain information only for as long as necessary to provide our services, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, enforce agreements, and maintain security. When information is no longer needed, we delete or anonymize it where reasonably possible.

6. Data Security

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational measures to protect information against unauthorized access, loss, misuse, alteration, or disclosure. However, no method of transmission or storage is completely secure.

7. User Choices and Rights

You may request access to, correction of, or deletion of your personal information by contacting us at support@rarefoot.com. You may also disconnect or revoke app permissions through your Meta, Facebook, Instagram, or Business account settings where applicable.

8. Data Deletion Instructions

To request deletion of your data associated with RareFoot, please email support@rarefoot.com with the subject line “Data Deletion Request”.

Please include the email address or account identifier associated with your use of our service. After verifying the request, we will delete or anonymize applicable personal data unless retention is required by law, security, fraud prevention, or legitimate business recordkeeping obligations.

9. Cookies and Similar Technologies

Our website or services may use cookies, logs, analytics tools, or similar technologies to operate the service, understand usage, improve functionality, and maintain security. You can control cookies through your browser settings.

10. Children’s Privacy

Our services are not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us so we can take appropriate action.

11. International Users

Your information may be processed and stored in countries other than your country of residence. Where required, we take appropriate steps to protect personal information in accordance with applicable law.

12. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated “Last updated” date.
